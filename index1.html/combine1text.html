Little Astronauts

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside of our solar system. While our solar system has eight planets orbiting the Sun, exoplanets orbit other stars far away in space. Scientists discover these planets using special telescopes and techniques, often by detecting the tiny dimming of a star's light when a planet passes in front of it. Some exoplanets are small and rocky, like Earth, while others are giant gas planets, like Jupiter. They come in many different sizes and temperatures, depending on how far they are from their stars.

One exciting thing about exoplanets is that some might have the right conditions to support life. Scientists are particularly interested in planets that are located in the "habitable zone" around their stars, where it's not too hot and not too cold, meaning liquid water could exist. Studying exoplanets helps us understand more about how planets form and whether there could be other life in the universe.
